Joost Wardenier

Welcome my webpage! As of September 2018, I am a second-year master student at the Leiden Observatory, specializing in Astronomical Instrumentation. My main research interests are the characterisation of exoplanets, high-contrast imaging and radio astronomy. While studying for my bachelor's degrees in physics and astronomy in Leiden, I worked on wavefront sensing and adaptive optics in the group of Christoph Keller and Sebastiaan Haffert. Last year, I conducted a research project under the supervision of Ignas Snellen and Paul MolliÃ¨re, in which I developed and tested a parametric cloud model for atmospheric retrievals. A first-authored paper on the results is under construction. Currently, I am working on optical interferometry and holographic aperture masking, in close collaboration with Frans Snik and David Doelman. It is thrilling to be part of a science that evolves so rapidly, boosted by continuous advancements in observational technology, modelling and data analysis techniques. Ground-breaking discoveries are just a matter of time.
